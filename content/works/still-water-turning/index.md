## Program Notes

*Still Water, Turning* begins with a chord that is less a theme than a place. The pianist returns to it repeatedly, listening for the changes produced by register, decay, and the pressure of intervening silence.

The three studies are performed without pause. Each one narrows the available musical material while enlarging the role of resonance. What appears to repeat is continuously altered by what remains sounding.

## Performance Note

The indicated durations should be treated as proportions rather than fixed measurements. Pedaling may be adjusted to the instrument and room, with clarity of decay taking priority over absolute consistency.
