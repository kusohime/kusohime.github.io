      <section aria-labelledby="impossible-vows-original">
        <h3 id="impossible-vows-original" class="poem-diptych__label">
          <span lang="en">Original</span>
          <span aria-hidden="true"> / </span>
          <span lang="zh-Hant-TW">原文</span>
        </h3>
        <blockquote class="poetry no-indent" lang="zh-Hant-TW">
          <p>
            上邪！<br />
            我欲與君相知，<br />
            長命無絕衰。<br />
            山無陵，<br />
            江水為竭，<br />
            冬雷震震，<br />
            夏雨雪，<br />
            天地合，<br />
            乃敢與君絕！
          </p>
        </blockquote>
      </section>
      <section aria-labelledby="impossible-vows-translation">
        <h3 id="impossible-vows-translation" class="poem-diptych__label">

          <span lang="zh-Hant-TW">英譯</span>
        </h3>
        <blockquote class="poetry no-indent" lang="en">
          <p>
            By Heaven!<br />
            I long for us to know each other,<br />
            through a long life, without parting or fading.<br />
            When mountains have lost their peaks,<br />
            when rivers have run dry,<br />
            when thunder rolls in winter,<br />
            when snow falls in summer,<br />
            when heaven and earth close into one—<br />
            only then would I dare<br />
            to part from you.
          </p>
        </blockquote>

<aside class="poem-diptych__coda" aria-labelledby="impossible-vows-coda">
  <p class="poem-diptych__label no-indent">
    <span lang="en">Why these two</span>
    <span aria-hidden="true"> / </span>
    <span lang="zh-Hant-TW">何以並置</span>
  </p>
  <h2 id="impossible-vows-coda">
    <span lang="en">The answering line</span>
    <span class="poem-diptych__translated-title" lang="zh-Hant-TW">應答之聲</span>
  </h2>
  <p class="no-indent" lang="en">Both poems place an impossible promise beside the pressure of time. The Han singer piles impossibility upon impossibility so that separation can never occur. Auden lets a lover make a similarly boundless claim, then gives Time the answering voice. Together they form a call and response: love speaks in absolutes; mortality replies—and neither voice wholly silences the other.</p>
  <p class="no-indent" lang="zh-Hant-TW">兩首詩都把不可能的誓言置於時間的壓力之下。《上邪》層層列出絕不可能發生的景象，以此斷言永不分離；奧登則先讓戀人說出無邊的承諾，再讓「時間」出聲回應。並置閱讀，它們彷彿一唱一和：愛情使用絕對的語言，有限生命提出反駁，而兩種聲音都沒有徹底壓倒對方。</p>
</aside>
