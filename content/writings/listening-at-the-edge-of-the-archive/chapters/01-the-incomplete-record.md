An archive does not simply preserve sound. It preserves decisions about what may count as sound, what may be described, and what can remain unnamed. The missing recording is therefore not empty. It is surrounded by catalogues, correspondence, testimony, and the habits of listeners who expected it to exist.[^archive]

To listen at the edge of an archive is to attend to this surrounding material without pretending that it restores what has been lost. One might describe the surviving trace with the deliberately simple function $a(t)$, where presence is uneven rather than binary.

$$
A = \int_{t_0}^{t_1} a(t)\,dt
$$

The displayed equation is a typesetting test, not an analytical claim. It lets mathematical notation sit within an otherwise prose-driven chapter.

[^archive]: For a general discussion of archival description, see the [Society of American Archivists](https://www2.archivists.org/).
