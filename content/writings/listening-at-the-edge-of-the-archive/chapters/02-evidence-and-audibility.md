Musical evidence often arrives in forms that cannot be heard directly. A rehearsal schedule implies repeated labor. A marked score preserves the pressure of an interpretation. A review records one listener's account while excluding the room in which that account was formed.

These materials ask for a practice of listening that is neither strictly sonic nor merely metaphorical. They require attention to relations among documents, bodies, and expectations.
