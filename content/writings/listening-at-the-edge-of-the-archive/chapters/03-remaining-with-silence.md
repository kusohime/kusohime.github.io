---
title: "Remaining with Silence"
slug: "remaining-with-silence"
excerpt: "Why absence should be described rather than automatically filled."
order: 3
---

Silence becomes unhelpful when it is treated as a single condition. Some silences are imposed; some are protective; some result from neglect; others are produced by the limits of a medium.

The task is not to fill every absence. It is to describe the structures around it carefully enough that absence is not mistaken for insignificance.

The Chinese term <ruby>留白<rp>(</rp><rt>liú bái</rt><rp>)</rp></ruby> offers one possible point of comparison: an intentionally retained blank, not merely an accidental lack.
