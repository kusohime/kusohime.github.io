*Still Water, Turning* begins from the premise that a repeated harmony never returns to the same acoustic situation. The piano retains traces: sympathetic vibration, pedal noise, uneven decay, and the listener's memory of what has just ended.

The work's three connected studies reduce their material gradually. As the number of events decreases, the differences among repetitions become more exposed. Silence is not a pause between objects but part of their changing definition.

The title describes no particular landscape. It names a contradiction that shaped the composition: apparent stillness containing continuous movement.

The relation may be sketched as $s_{n+1}=s_n+\varepsilon_n$, where each return retains a small acoustic remainder.[^note]
