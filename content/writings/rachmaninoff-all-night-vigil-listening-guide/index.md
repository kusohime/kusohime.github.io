This is a quick note slash listening guide to the All-Night Vigil. I was not able to find which recording the timestamps are for, but I will add that back once it comes to me.

The Church Slavonic titles reproduce the opening words of each movement in the old orthography. To modernize them, replace **і** with **и** and **ѣ** with **е**, and remove final **ъ**.

## Vespers

### I. Пріидите поклонимся · Come, Let Us Worship

**5:55–8:25**

### II. Благослови душе моя Господа · Bless the Lord, O My Soul

**8:28–13:30**

Psalm 104. The opening “Amen” is omitted here.

### III. Блаженъ мужъ · Blessed Is the Man

**16:50–22:05**

Psalms 1–3; this movement uses text only from Psalm 1.

### IV. Свѣте тихій · Gladsome Light

**28:05–31:28**

Drawn from Psalm 141.

### V. Нынѣ отпущаеши · Lord, Now Lettest Thou

**40:00–43:07**

Luke 2:29–32. Its mounting climaxes and the contrabass low B-flat at the close have made it the best-known movement of the work.

### VI. Богородице Дѣво · Rejoice, O Virgin Theotokos

**44:28–47:02**

## Matins

### VII. Слава въ вышнихъ Богу (шестопсалміе) · Glory to God in the Highest (The Six Psalms)

**48:05–51:05**

### VIII. Хвалите имя Господне · Praise the Name of the Lord

**1:05:52–1:07:57**

Drawn from Psalm 136.

### IX. Благословенъ еси Господи · Blessed Art Thou, O Lord

**1:08:03–1:14:15**

### X. Воскресеніе Христово видѣвше · Having Beheld the Resurrection of Christ

**1:24:03–1:27:17**

### XI. Величитъ душа моя Господа · My Soul Magnifies the Lord

**1:48:27–1:55:06**

Luke 1:46–55.

### XII. Великое славословіе · The Great Doxology

**1:59:50–2:06:32**

### XIII. Тропарь. Днесь спасеніе. · Troparion: Today Salvation Has Come

**2:18:57–2:21:08**

### XIV. Тропарь. Воскресъ изъ гроба · Troparion: Thou Didst Rise from the Tomb

**2:06:38–2:10:30**

As the service fell in an even-numbered week, the Matins portion of the vigil concluded first with Movement XIV. After the metropolitan addressed the congregation and spoke about Rachmaninoff’s setting, Movement XIII was performed as an encore.
