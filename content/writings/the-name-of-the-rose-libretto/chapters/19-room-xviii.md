### <span class="libretto-source" lang="it" id="room-xviii">Stanza XVIII – Severino &lt;Mi bemolle&gt;</span><br /><span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">Room XVIII – Severino &lt;E-flat&gt;</span>

*<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">Severino si chiude nel laboratorio e, mentre si mette i guanti, vede Malachia.</span>*<br />
*<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">Severino locks himself in the laboratory and, as he puts on his gloves, sees Malachia.</span>*

**Severino**  

*<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">(preoccupato)</span>*<br />
*<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">(worried)</span>*

<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">Sei qui per le tue erbe?</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">Are you here for your herbs?</span>

**Malachia**  
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">E per cos’altro?</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">And for what else?</span>

**Severino**  
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">Se sei qui per questo,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">If you are here for this,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">un attimo, le preparo…</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">one moment, I will prepare them…</span>

*<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">(Severino si volta; Malachia afferra da un tavolo una sfera armillare e lo colpisce in testa)</span>*<br />
*<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">(Severino turns; Malachia seizes an armillary sphere from a table and strikes him on the head)</span>*

**Malachia**  
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">Dannato maiale,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">Damned pig,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">vai a sfamare i porci.</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">go feed the swine.</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">Ti sei preso il mio adorato,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">You took my beloved,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">l’hai sedotto e avvelenato;</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">you seduced and poisoned him;</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">l’hai comprato con un libro;</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">you bought him with a book;</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">quel libro maledetto!</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">that accursed book!</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">Porta sotto terra</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">Carry beneath the earth</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">le tue erbe disgustose!</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">your disgusting herbs!</span>

*<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">(Malachia si allontana con il libro lasciando la porta aperta. Dopo poco entra Remigio trafelato che, ignorando il cadavere, rovista dappertutto. Irrompe Malachia accompagnato degli arcieri e dal loro capitano)</span>*<br />
*<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">(Malachia moves away with the book, leaving the door open. Shortly afterward Remigio enters breathlessly and, ignoring the corpse, rummages everywhere. Malachia bursts in accompanied by the archers and their captain)</span>*

**Remigio**  
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">Malachia,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">Malachia,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">le mie carte, dove sono?</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">my papers, where are they?</span>

**Malachia**  
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">Quali carte? Qui c’è un morto!</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">What papers? There is a dead man here!</span>

*<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">(Entrano Abbone, Bertrando, d’Anneaux, Michele, Ubertino, Jorge, Guglielmo, Adso e altri monaci)</span>*<br />
*<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">(Abbone, Bertrando, d’Anneaux, Michele, Ubertino, Jorge, Guglielmo, Adso, and other monks enter)</span>*

**Abbone**  
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">Un altro morto!</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">Another dead man!</span>

**Ubertino**  
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">Maria Vergine!</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">Virgin Mary!</span>

**Bertrando, Michele, Jean d’Anneaux e altri monaci**  
<span class="libretto-source" lang="la">Miserere nobis, Domine!</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">Have mercy on us, Lord!</span>

**Jorge**  
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">L’Anticristo sta arrivando.</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">The Antichrist is coming.</span>

**Monaci**  
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">La bestia si aggira per l’Abbazia.</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">The beast roams through the Abbey.</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">La grande bestia che viene dal mare</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">The great beast that comes from the sea</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">ha sette teste con dieci corna,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">has seven heads with ten horns,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">e su ogni testa ha tre bestemmie,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">and on every head has three blasphemies,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">e su ogni corno porta un diadema.</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">and on every horn bears a diadem.</span>

**Guglielmo**  
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">Povero Severino…</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">Poor Severino…</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">Per timore di un veleno</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">For fear of a poison</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">hai indossato questi guanti</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">you put on these gloves</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">ma…</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">but…</span>

*<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">(guardando la sfera ammaccata)</span>*<br />
*<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">(looking at the dented sphere)</span>*

<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">Questa sfera armillare…</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">This armillary sphere…</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">Santo Cielo!</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">Good Heavens!</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">“E fu colpita</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">“And was struck</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">la terza parte del sole</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">the third part of the sun,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">della luna,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">of the moon,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">delle stelle.”</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">of the stars.”</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">La quarta tromba!</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">The fourth trumpet!</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">Prima la grandine</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">First the hail,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">poi il sangue</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">then the blood,</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">e dopo l’acqua</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">and after that the water</span><br />
<span class="libretto-source" lang="it">… ora le stelle.</span><br />
<span class="libretto-translation" lang="en">… now the stars.</span>
