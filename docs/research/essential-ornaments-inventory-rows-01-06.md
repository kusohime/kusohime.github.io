# Ornament inventory: rows 1-6

Source image inspected: `wesentliche-bands/rows-01-06.png`, checked against `wesentliche-manieren-full-400dpi.png`.

## Method and notation legend

- Scope: Tomás de Santa María, Girolamo Diruta, Michael Praetorius, John Playford, Jacques Champion de Chambonnières, and Jean Henry d'Anglebert.
- All seven chart families were checked: Vorschlag, Triller, Trillervarianten, Doppelschlag, Mordent, Mordent mit Vorhalt / schneller / Battement, and Schleifer.
- A separately barred alternative, an alternative introduced by `oder`, a separately named sign, or a separate staff line is counted as a distinct example. A continuous pedagogical excerpt remains one example even when it places the ornament several times; those internal occurrences are noted.
- Relative pitch uses `0` for the destination or main note, `+1` for the diatonic upper neighbor, `-1` for the lower neighbor, and so on. `x N` means repeat the bracketed cell N times. `ctx` means unornamented contextual notes. These normalized degrees do not preserve the source key, clef, accidentals, or octave.
- The small `=` printed above many examples is treated as the chart's division between a notated form/context and its realization, not as an ornament sign.
- Rhythms are described by visible proportional grouping rather than modern note names where the early notation or reproduction does not make an exact modern value secure.
- Confidence is about the relative-pitch transcription, not the existence of the example. No readable example has been omitted because its pitches are uncertain.

## Ambiguities requiring source-level verification before definitive MEI

1. **Santa María term mapping:** the chart places `Quiebro` and `Reyterado` beside one upper staff line, so the three upper-line alternatives cannot be assigned one-to-one to those two names from this chart alone.
2. **Santa María inner notes:** SM-TR-03, SM-TR-06, and the Redoble terminations have merged noteheads/stems. Their contours are usable for a provisional rendering, but exact pitch arrays should be checked in the 1565 source.
3. **Diruta composite exercises:** Accento, Tremoli, Groppi, and Clamationi are continuous excerpts rather than isolated sign/equation pairs. Encoding them as whole snippets is more faithful than splitting every local occurrence into an invented standalone ornament.
4. **Praetorius Accentus:** the rising-third line visibly has three realizations after the plain interval, but only one printed `oder`; PR-VO-03 through PR-VO-05 should remain labelled as chart alternatives rather than given stronger source terminology.
5. **Playford Elevation/Cadent:** both names share one upper staff line. Crop the first half for Elevation and the second half for Cadent, but verify the division against Playford before claiming exact terminal pitches.
6. **d'Anglebert Cadence signs:** the two Cadence and two Double Cadence forms are visibly separate, while their dense inner trill notes are too crowded for a note-perfect reading from this reproduction.
7. **d'Anglebert Coulé segmentation:** the Schleifer cell contains five visible equation units under two printed names. This inventory keeps all five; DA-SL-03 and DA-SL-05 require the original engraved table for exact rhythmic spacing.
